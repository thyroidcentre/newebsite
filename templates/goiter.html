Goiter

A goiter is an irregular growth of the thyroid gland. A goiter can be an overall enlargement of the thyroid( diffuse goiter), or it may be the result of the irregular growth of multiple nodules within the thyroid gland (multi nodular goiter). A goiter may be associated with an abnormal decrease or increase in the production of thyroid hormones.

What are the causes of a goiter?

1 . Iodine deficiency - Historically iodine deficiency was the commonest cause for thyroid enlargement. If a person does not get enough dietary iodine, the thyroid gland becomes enlarged in an effort to make adequate amounts of thyroid hormone. However iodine deficiency has now become uncommon, following the widespread use of iodised salt.

2 . Hashimoto’s disease - Hashimoto’s disease is an autoimmune disease, where the body’s own immune system attacks the thyroid gland. The damaged and inflamed thyroid gland then becomes swollen and does not produce adequate thyroid hormone.

3 . Graves’ disease - Graves’ disease is another autoimmune disease where the immune system produces antibodies that stimulate the thyroid gland. This causes the thyroid gland to become enlarged and produce excess thyroid hormone.

4 . Thyroid nodules - A nodule is an irregular growth of cells forming a lump. A person can have a single thyroid or multiple nodules( multi nodular goiter). Most thyroid nodules are non cancerous. They are the result of various factors including genetics, environment and diet. No particular cause can be identified in many of the nodules.

5. Thyroid cancer - About 5 to 10 % of people with thyroid nodules have thyroid cancer. The common forms of thyroid cancer have safe and effective treatments

6. Subacute thyroiditis - This is an inflammation of the thyroid gland which can be accompanied by fever and pain over the throat.

How is a goiter evaluated?

A goiter can frequently be first identified during a routine health checkup. A doctor can identify a goiter by examining your neck.


Investigations are then done to look for hormone levels, nodules in the thyroid and to identify the nature of the nodule. Tests usually include the following

1 . Thyroid function tests - A blood test is done to measure thyroid function. TSH,T4 and T3 levels will tell us if the thyroid gland is producing abnormally low or high levels of thyroid hormone.

2 . Antibody test - If the results of the thyroid function tests are abnormal, your doctor might order antibody tests such as the Anti TPO antibody and the TSH receptor antibody.

3 . Ultrasound - An ultrasound measures the exact size of the goiter, and is useful to detect any abnormal characteristics which might be of concern.

4 . FNAC ( Fine needle aspiration cytology) - This procedure is also known as the needle test. During the FNAC an ultrasound is used to guide a small needle into the suspicious area of the thyroid, to obtain a tissue sample. This sample is studied under a microscope for signs of cancer.

5 . Radioactive iodine uptake / Tc 99 uptake - Some patients may need a special scan where small amounts of radioactive material are used look for special patterns of thyroid uptake. The results may help determine the cause of the goiter.

How is a goiter treated?

Treatment depends on the cause of the goiter, the size of the goiter and the symptoms it has caused.


If the goiter is small, thyroid function is normal, with the ultrasound and FNAC test reporting a benign cause your doctor might choose to keep you on follow up, with periodic blood tests and ultrasound scans.


1 . Medications - If increased or decreased thyroid function is found on the thyroid hormone tests, medications will be prescribed to normalise hormone levels. Other causes for a goiter such as thyroiditis may need other medications .

2 . Surgery - Surgery may be advised in bigger goiters for relief of obstruction to the windpipe, food pipe etc. Surgery will be needed if suspicious features or ,features of thyroid cancer are detected on other tests. Patients who have an obviously visible goiter may need surgery for cosmetic reasons.

3 . Non surgical options - patients with noncancerous goiters can also consider the option of non surgical procedures such as microwave ablation and radio frequency ablation.
