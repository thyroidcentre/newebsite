Microwave & Radiofrequency Ablation

Home Our Services Microwave & Radiofrequency Ablation

A no scar no hypothyroidism solution

Thyroid nodules are one of the most common thyroid disorders, and are estimated to affect 4 to 7% of the population. Fortunately, about 95% of all thyroid nodules are non cancerous and only 4 to 6.5% are malignant. Traditionally, surgery has been has been the primary approach to treating thyroid nodules. However surgery comes with the limitations of postoperative permanent hypothyroidism, surgical scars and possibility of recurrent laryngeal nerve paralysis.

Image guided thermal ablation is a recent innovation in the non-operative management of thyroid nodules. Thermal ablation techniques include microwave ablation(MWA) and radiofrequency ablation(RFA). These procedures are performed under ultrasound guidance by an expert and selectively target the thyroid nodule without affecting the surrounding normal thyroid tissue. Many studies have confirmed that these techniques are effective in reducing nodule sizes and compressive symptoms, with good cosmetic results. Additionally, these procedures do not require general anesthesia, leave no scars and cause no postoperative pain, allowing patients to be discharged the same day. On the basis of robust evidence, thermal ablation(RFA,MWA) is now proposed as a first-line treatment option for selected patients by the Korean Health Authorities , the American Association of Clinical Endocrinologists, the Italian Association of Clinical Endocrinologists , and the European Thyroid Association.

What is microwave ablation?

Microwave ablation is a minimally invasive technique for the treatment of thyroid nodules. It applies the principle of using microwave energy to heat and coagulate tumor tissue. A specialized probe is inserted into the tumor tissue under ultrasound guidance. This probe is used to emit controlled microwave energy at the target site to selectively destroy the tumor, while preserving surrounding normal thyroid tissue.

What are the advantages of microwave ablation?

Microwave ablation offers several benefits when compared to thyroid surgery. The procedure is performed with a small incision only for the probe, and is usually performed under local anaesthesia. This offers the benefit of quicker recovery and reduced pain . A permanent scar is avoided. As the thyroid nodule is selectively targeted, normal function of the rest of the thyroid gland is usually preserved. Which nodules are suitable for microwave ablation?

Microwave ablation is now considered an effective alternative to surgery for the management of benign thyroid nodules, whether solid, cystic, functional or nonfunctional. It is, however an operator-dependent technique and should be performed in centers with specific expertise. A careful clinical, functional and radiological assessment of the nature of the nodule(ultrasound/FNAC) and its location in relation to the trachea, carotid artery etc is paramount prior to the procedure. The selection of the patients should be rigorous because the nodule size and the structural and functional characteristics influence the appropriateness and the outcomes of the treatment. Microwave ablation can also be used in selected patients with parathyroid adenomas, and in some non invasive thyroid cancers.

What is the success rate of microwave ablation?

A reduction in nodule size of 60 to 90%, within 6-12 months can be expected. More than 90% of patients report relief from symptoms like compression and visible swelling. The recurrence rate is low(5-10%) with some nodules requiring a second ablation.

Are there any risks associated with microwave ablation?

The likelihood of risks is low with this procedure. In some cases the nodule may regrow, years after the procedure. Rare complications can occur, as with any procedure and may include nodule rupture, decreased thyroid function, bleeding, or infection.
